In the rapidly evolving blockchain industry, Transaction Per Second (TPS) is a critical metric that defines the performance and scalability of blockchain networks. Many blockchain projects face inherent TPS limitations that affect their real-world usability and user experience. ## TPS and User Experience: Core Comparisons

### The Importance of TPS in Blockchain

TPS measures the number of transactions a blockchain can process in a single second. A higher TPS indicates a more scalable and efficient network, capable of handling a larger volume of transactions without delays or congestion. This is crucial for applications requiring fast and reliable transaction processing, such as decentralized finance (DeFi), gaming, and supply chain management.

### Industry-Wide TPS Limitations

Many blockchain networks struggle with TPS limitations that hinder their performance and user experience. These limitations often result in network congestion, high transaction fees, and slow confirmation times, especially during peak usage periods. The discrepancy between theoretical and real-world TPS also highlights the challenges in maintaining high performance across different environments.

Key limitations include:
- **Network Congestion:** Low TPS can lead to significant congestion during high demand periods, causing delays in transaction processing and negatively impacting user experience.
- **High Transaction Fees:** Network congestion often drives up transaction fees, making transactions costly, particularly for microtransactions or high-volume activities.
- **Scalability Issues:** Limited TPS hampers a network’s ability to scale, restricting support for a growing user base and complex applications.
- **Performance Discrepancies:** The gap between theoretical maximum TPS and actual performance in real-world conditions is influenced by network architecture, consensus mechanisms, and real-time processing capabilities.
